What is Christmas?

Christmas is a Christian holiday that celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ. Christmas is also a cultural holiday for many non-Christians.
Christmas is celebrated on the 25th day of December. It is one of the most important days of the year for Christians.
The Christmas season (called Christmastide) ends 6 January, also called the Twelfth Day of Christmas. On this day, Christians celebrate Epiphany. People celebrate Christmas in many places around the world. Some people celebrate Christmas as a religious holiday.
Christmas traditions are different in different countries. However, they almost always include a feast; giving gifts or cards; and enjoying church or public festivities, like singing Christmas carols and songs. The idea of Santa Claus is based on Saint Nicholas, a 4th-century Christian saint. In the Northern Hemisphere, Christmastide happens during the winter.
